Health Insurance
Please note: If this is your first semester at Pace University, you have the option to waive the mandatory university's health insurance coverage b/c of your coverage via VA health benefits (Tricare) or any other private coverage you may have. All full time undergraduate and graduate student veterans must have health insurance. Student veterans will automatically be enrolled on the school accident/sickness plan when they register. You may waive the school's insurance if you provide comparable proof of coverage. Please sign onto Pace Portal and click the Student tab. By scrolling down and viewing the left side options, you can select Health Insurance Waiver. Click on this link and sign in again, which will open the site and enable you to complete the required information in order to Waive Out of the Student Health Insurance offering. Should you have any questions or concerns regarding health insurance please state them in the comment entry field at the end of this form and an OSA VA Team representative will be in touch with you shortly. If you are a returning/continuing student and have outside health insurance you must complete the electronic waiver found in your Pace Portal prior to the start of the Fall semester.

You are eligible for the Yellow Ribbon Program if you are 100% covered under the Post 9/11 GI Bill (CH. 33). To be 100% covered under the Post 9/11 GI Bill, you must have served at least three years of aggregate active duty service after September 11, 2001 and have been honorably discharged. PLEASE NOTE: Veterans on Active Duty Status and spouses of veterans using GI BILL benefits while their veteran spouse is on Active Duty Status are NOT eligible for funds from the Yellow Ribbon Program nor are they eligible for CH 33's Basic Allowance for Housing.

Meal Plan
Commuting undergraduate on-campus student veteran beneficiaries have the option to keep the university's $160.00/semester commuter meal plan. Federal GI BILL benefits are tuition/fees specific, therefore will NOT cover this expenditure. Should you wish to keep this meal plan, the VA considers you to use your BAH to remit this charge.

Check here if you would like to keep the meal plan

Please note: The Commuter Meal Plan is mandatory for all students living in Pace's sponsored Residence Halls.
Immunization Compliance
All student veterans matriculated in a degree program, enrolled for 6 or more credits, and participating in classes on a campus at Pace University must comply with the New York State Department of Health immunization requirements for admission (and continuation) as a Pace student detailed below.

Student veterans enrolled solely in online courses are not required to be in compliance. Once registered for a course requiring attendance on campus, these students must meet all requirements.
For more detailed information about the MMR process please visit: http://www.pace.edu/office-student-assistance/immunization

Sallie Mae Payment Plan For Remaining Balance (if applicable)- Should a tuition/fees balance exist after the application of your VA/GI BILL educational benefits, it must be remitted in FULL prior to each semester's balance payment deadline to avoid incurring the university's late fee. Similarly should you be residing in the campus dormitories, you are responsible to remit the room/board related charges in full prior to each semester's balance payment deadline to be permitted to move into the dorms and not incur the late fee. This may be accomplished either by paying out of pocket, accepting loans, or enrolling in the university's 0% interest payment plan offered through Sallie Mae. It is prohibited to pay the room/board related charges on a semester-by-semester basis directly upon receiving your GI BILL educational benefit's monthly basic allowance for housing (BAH). All room/board charges must be paid up front prior to moving in.
